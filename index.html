Building the Business

ClaycrazE is formalized as an LLC with the City of Chesapeake business license and an IRS EIN. Here’s the practical side of getting a ceramics studio set up right.

LLC

Registered with the Virginia SCC on June 11, 2019. Why an LLC? Liability protection and a clean foundation for growth.

Tax ID (EIN)

IRS EIN obtained for banking, invoicing, and tax filings. Keeps business and personal cleanly separated.

License

City of Chesapeake retail merchant license in place. Local compliance matters — and supports the arts ecosystem.

Quick checklist
  • Choose a business name and register LLC
  • Get EIN for taxes and banking
  • Open dedicated business bank account
  • Obtain local business license
  • Simple operating agreement (optional but helpful)

The Home Studio

Why home? Focus, flexibility, and a workflow that fits how I build. Public studios are great — stocked, maintained, and fired by studio elves — but the home studio is the right call for this season.

Skutt 1027-3 Kiln

Purchased from Clayworks (Richmond, VA). Reliable, roomy, and perfect for larger bonsai containers and stoneware.

Monitoring & Safety

Thermocouples + logging planned; safe kiln placement and ventilation are part of the build plan.

Colonial Clay (Yorktown)

Yorktown clay is part of the story — historically used by William Rogers, the “Poor Potter.” It’s not perfect straight from the ground, so I document the small amendments that make it sing.

From ground to wheel

Processing, wedging, and test tiles through a range of cones to dial in shrinkage, absorption, and color.

Amendments

  • Screening & deflocculation tests
  • Grogs/sand for structure
  • Flux tweaks for vitrification
